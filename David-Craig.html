David Craig
David Craig works with abandoned objects and discarded apparatuses using a combination of industrial and natural processes to reconsider intended function as a site for exploration. The resulting forms are often unfixed in position and appearance, presenting themselves with sincere absurdity. By examining the human relationship to machines and their unholy collision with natural systems, we are asked to question ideas of value and purpose in a world that will one day forget us. David draws from past experiences as a wildland firefighter, polar laborer, and cross country driver, to make work that embraces its own inability to recite a complete story. 1989.
