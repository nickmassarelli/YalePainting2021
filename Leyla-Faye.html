Exhibition Artists
Leyla Faye
Leyla Faye (b. 1994, Minneapolis) sees her paintings as an environment in which to unpack the entangled narratives of the Black/white racial binary in the United States. As the child of a biracial family, she is interested in the confrontation of white adjacency as a destabilizing yet necessary discourse. In an attempt to blur the lines of their segregated realities, the figures in her paintings play tug of war between whimsy and melancholy. And through scenes of jumbled relationships, she uses the mixture of tenderness and satire to address the historically intimate, yet alienating relationship, this country has to the Black subject.
